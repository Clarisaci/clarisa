About

Bridging the bench and the terminal.

My work sits at the intersection of science, technology, and student advocacy. I began my career in analytical chemistry and environmental research at Oregon State University’s Food Safety and Environmental Stewardship Program (FSES), where I worked across laboratory operations, mass spectrometry workflows, regulatory compliance, and laboratory information systems.

Today, I’m pursuing a Master of Engineering in Computer Science with a focus on Cybersecurity and a minor in Artificial Intelligence. My interests center on secure systems, infrastructure, machine learning, and the intersection of technology and human decision-making.

Alongside my graduate studies, I serve as Coordinator of Student Outreach & Scholarships for Oregon State University’s College of Agricultural Sciences, where I advise the MANRRS and SACNAS chapters, support scholarship programs, and lead outreach initiatives focused on student success, leadership development, and inclusive STEM communities.

Over the past two years, I helped grow chapter membership by more than 170% through intentional outreach, inclusive programming, and community-building initiatives. I also manage approximately $300,000 in scholarship support, coordinate partnerships with industry and nonprofit organizations, and assist students with internships, grants, research opportunities, and career preparation.
